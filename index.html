Adolf Hitler

The man who killed a million lives

Here's a time line of Hitler's life:

  • 1889 - Born in Braunau am Inn, Austria-Hungary (present-day Austria).
  • 1907 - Moves to Vienna to pursue his dream of becoming an artist. Rejected twice from the Vienna Academy of Fine Arts.
  • 1913 - Moves to Munich, Germany.
  • 1914 - Enlists in the German army during World War I. Decorated for bravery.
  • 1919 - Discharged from the army and deeply affected by Germany's defeat. Joins the German Workers' Party (DAP), which later becomes the Nazi Party.
  • 1912 - Becomes leader of the Nazi Party.
  • 1923 - Leads the failed Beer Hall Putsch, a coup attempt in Munich. Imprisoned for a year, during which he writes Mein Kampf (My Struggle), outlining his Nazi ideology.
  • 1924 - Released from prison and begins rebuilding the Nazi Party.
  • 1932 - The Nazi Party becomes the largest party in the German parliament (Reichstag).
  • 1933 - Appointed Chancellor of Germany by President Hindenburg. Begins consolidating power and establishing a totalitarian dictatorship.
  • 1934 - Orders the killing of political rivals in the Night of the Long Knives.
  • 1935 - Introduces the Nuremberg Laws, which discriminate against Jews and other minorities.
  • 1936 - Remilitarizes the Rhineland, violating the Treaty of Versailles.
  • 1938 - Orchestrates the Anschluss, the annexation of Austria by Germany. Encourages violent attacks against Jews on Kristallnacht (Night of Broken Glass).
  • 1939 - Invades Poland, sparking World War II.
  • 1941 - Orders the invasion of the Soviet Union.
  • 1942 - The Holocaust, the systematic extermination of Jews and other minorities, reaches its peak.
  • 1945 - As Allied forces close in, Hitler commits suicide in his Berlin bunker. Germany surrenders, ending World War II in Europe.
